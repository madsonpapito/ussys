Types of Social Security Benefits

Retirement Benefits
  • Available for workers 62+
  • Amount based on 35 highest-earning years
Disability Benefits (SSDI)
  • For those who cannot work due to health
  • Requires medical proof of long-term disability
Survivor Benefits
  • For widows, widowers, and children
  • Protecting families after a worker's death
SSI Benefits
  • For low-income and limited resources
  • Based on financial need, not work history

Retirement Benefits: Maximize Your Income

Retirement Eligibility Requirements

Born 1943-1954: FRA 66
Born 1955-1959: FRA 66 + Mo
Born 1960+: FRA 67
Work History: 40 Credits

How Benefits Are Calculated

  • Lifetime Earnings

    SSA looks at your top 35 years of indexed earnings.

  • Claiming Timing

    Claiming early (62) reduces Payout; claiming late (70) increases it.

Disability Benefits: When You Can't Work

Eligibility Requirements
  • Condition must last 1+ year or result in death
  • Cannot perform "Substantial Gainful Activity"
  • Work credits requirement based on your age
Application Process
  • 1
    Apply Online/Phone

    DDS Review

    Decision Phase

Pro Tips to Maximize Your Benefits

Check Records Annually

Verify your earnings history on SSA.gov to avoid missing credits.

Spousal Coordination

Maximize family income by coordinating when each spouse claims.

Check for Survivor Payouts

Ex-spouses may be eligible for benefits if married for 10+ years.

Keep Resources Low for SSI

Keep countable assets below $2k (individual) or $3k (couple).

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Claiming too early without needing the money immediately.
Missing out on spousal benefits you are legally owed.
Not checking earnings history for errors that lower payouts.
Working while claiming early (exceeding earnings limits).
