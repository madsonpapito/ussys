Types of Housing Assistance Programs

Section 8 Voucher
  • Most common assistance program
  • You find your own housing
Public Housing
  • Owned by the government
  • Rent based on income
Project-Based Vouchers
  • Linked to specific buildings
  • Transfers only after 1 year
Emergency Housing
  • For homelessness crisis
  • Immediate short-term aid

Section 8 Housing Choice Voucher

As the largest federal housing assistance program, it provides vouchers that allow you to choose your own housing in the private market. You pay 30% of your income toward rent, and the voucher covers the rest.

How to Apply for Housing Assistance

Required Documents

  • Social Security cards for all household members
  • Birth Certificates
  • Drivers license or photo ID
  • Proof of all income (pay stubs)
  • Bank Statements
  • Utility bill verification

Application Steps

Find Your Local Housing Authority

Locate the office in the county you want to live in.

Submit Application

Complete application forms by mail or in person.

Wait for Response

Your application will be placed on the waitlist.

Tips to Get Housing Assistance Faster

Apply with Multiple Offices

Apply to several housing authorities at the same time.

Special Preferences

Ensure you identify if you are disabled, elderly, or homeless.

Stay in Contact

Keep your address and phone number updated with the HA.

Look for Project-Based

Specific buildings may have shorter wait times than vouchers.
