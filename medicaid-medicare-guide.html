Medicaid vs Medicare: What's the Difference?

Medicaid
  • For Low-Income individuals and families
  • State/Federal Program (Varies by state)
  • Broad Coverage with low/no costs
  • Assets/Income thresholds apply
Medicare
  • For Seniors 65+ and those with disabilities
  • Federal Program (Same nationwide)
  • Defined parts (A, B, C, D)
  • Ages 65+ and ESRD/ALS disability

Medicaid: Free Health Insurance

Medicaid provides health coverage to millions of Americans, including eligible low-income adults, children, pregnant women, elderly adults and people with disabilities. It is the largest source of health coverage in the US.

    Medicare Eligibility & Enrollment

    Automatic Enrollment
    • Ages 65 and already receiving SS or RRB
    • Disability - after 24 months of disability benefits
    • ALS/ESRD - automatic at time of diagnosis
    Enrollment Periods
    • Initial (7 Months): 3 months before to 3 after age 65
    • General (Jan-Mar): For missed Initial period
    • Special: Based on life events (job loss/move)
    • Open (Oct 15 - Dec 7): Switch plans annually

    Ways to Save Money

  • Extra Help (LIS)

    Get help with Part D drug costs (FPL limits apply).

    If qualified for both, Medicaid covers your Medicare costs.

  • Compare Plans

    Check Part C (Advantage) plans annually via Medicare.gov.

    • Pro Tips to Maximize Your Benefits

    Don't Miss Enrollment Deadlines

    Missing Part B or Part D initial enrollment can lead to permanent penalties.

    Compare Plans Annually

    Review your coverage during the Open Enrollment period (Oct 15 - Dec 7).

    Keep Medical Records

    Organize your medical paperwork and expenses for easier applications.

    Use Free Resources

    Use resources like Medicare.gov or SHIP state-provided counseling.

    Common Mistakes to Avoid

    Missing the enrollment deadlines, resulting in lifelong premium surcharges.
    Assuming Medicare covers everything (it doesn't cover dental, vision, or long-term care).
    Not checking if your preferred doctors are in your plan's network.
    Not reporting life changes (income/household) which affects Medicaid eligibility.
