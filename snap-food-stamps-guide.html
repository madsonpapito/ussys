What is SNAP?

The Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) is a federal program that provides monthly benefits to help low-income families buy food. Benefits are loaded onto an Electronic Benefits Transfer (EBT) card, which works like a debit card at grocery stores.

Key Benefits
  • Monthly food assistance up to $1,751 for large families
  • Accepted at most grocery stores, farmers markets, and online retailers
  • No minimum cost for benefits
  • Same day access in urgent cases (Expedited SNAP)

How to Apply for SNAP

1. Online Overview

Visit your state's agency website to find the application portal for SNAP benefits.

2. Apply Online or In-Person

Complete the application with your income history and household details.

3. Complete Interview

Most states require a phone interview to verify your identity and income.

IMPORTANT: DO NOT MISS YOUR SCHEDULED INTERVIEW.
4. Receive EBT Card

If approved, your benefits will be loaded onto an EBT card mailed to your home address.

YOUR BENEFITS WILL BE RE-LOADED MONTHLY.

Where Can You Use SNAP?

What You Can Buy
  • Fruits and Vegetables
  • Meat, Poultry and Fish
  • Dairy products
  • Breads and Cereals
  • Seeds and plants for food
What You Cannot Buy
  • Alcohol or Tobacco
  • Vitamins or Medicines
  • Hot Prepared Food
  • Pet Food
  • Cleaning Supplies

Pro Tips to Maximize Your Benefits

Shop at Farmers Markets

Many markets offer "Double Up Food Bucks" where your SNAP money counts twice.

Use Online Shopping

Major retailers like Amazon and Walmart accept EBT cards for online food orders.

Check Balance Regularly

Use the mobile app to keep an eye on your benefits and expiring dates.

Report Household Changes

Failure to report a change in income or household size can lead to disqualification.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not reporting income changes within 10 days of the change.
  • Providing inaccurate information regarding the number of residents.
  • Missing your interview time which causes an immediate denial.
  • Using someone else's EBT card which is considered federal fraud.
